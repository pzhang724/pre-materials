## Section Breakdown

### 1. Opening & Motivation
- Why pharma + AI now?
- Scale of the problem: cost, time, attrition rates in drug development
- The GenAI shift: from prediction to generation

### 2. GenAI Primer
- What is generative AI? (LLMs, diffusion models, graph neural nets)
- How it differs from traditional ML
- Key modalities relevant to pharma: text, molecules, images, multimodal

### 3. Drug Discovery
- Target identification & validation (literature mining, protein structure)
- De novo molecule generation (SMILES, graph-based models)
- Virtual screening & ADMET property prediction
- AlphaFold 2/3 and structural biology

### 4. Clinical Development
- Trial design optimization
- Patient recruitment & eligibility matching
- Synthetic control arms
- Real-world evidence (RWE) generation

### 5. Regulatory & Safety
- AI-assisted medical writing (CSRs, briefing documents)
- Adverse event detection & signal mining
- FDA/EMA stance on AI-generated submissions
- Validation, traceability, explainability requirements

### 6. Real-World Examples
- Insilico Medicine — AI-designed drug to Phase II
- BenevolentAI — target discovery
- Pfizer/AstraZeneca GenAI partnerships
- Internal use cases: chatbots on clinical data, document Q&A

### 7. Challenges & Risks
- Hallucination and factual reliability
- Data privacy (patient data, IP)
- Validation & regulatory acceptance
- Talent gap and change management

### 8. Outlook & Summary
- Near-term (1–2 yr): productivity tools, writing automation
- Mid-term (3–5 yr): AI-designed candidates in Phase III
- Long-term: autonomous discovery pipelines
- Key takeaways

---

## Key Messages

1. GenAI is already in pharma pipelines — not a future technology.
2. The biggest near-term wins are in productivity: writing, search, synthesis.
3. Discovery applications are real but require rigorous validation.
4. Regulatory frameworks are evolving — engage early.
